- **Photos/Videos:** Add before-after case studies, facility tours, testimonials (video = 3x higher engagement)

**Priority 2: Local Citation Building**
- Claim profiles on: Physio Inq, Localsearch, Healthgrades, HealthEngine
- Ensure NAP consistency across all platforms
- Add business description mentioning SCI + disability specialization
- **Cost Impact:** Low (mostly free or <$50/platform)

**Priority 3: Local Content Pages**
Create dedicated landing pages for high-intent local searches:
- "Exercise Physiology Services Gold Coast"
- "SCI Rehabilitation Gold Coast" (target Making Strides' territory)
- "Exercise Physiology for [Condition] on the Gold Coast" (MS, stroke, amputation, CP, TBI)
- "NDIS Exercise Physiology Gold Coast"
- "Online Exercise Physiology Services (Queensland + NSW)"

Each page should:
- Include local landmarks ("near [suburb]" references)
- Feature practitioner credentials + photos
- Explain NDIS/funding options
- Include CTAs (booking link, contact form, phone)
- 300-500 words minimum (Google prefers depth)

**Impact:** Research shows adding local landmarks increases CTR by 22% vs. postal codes alone.

### 2.2 Niche-Focused Keyword Strategy (National Authority)

**Lesson from Physio & Exercise Lab:** Avoid head-to-head battles for "exercise physiologist Brisbane/Sydney" — instead, dominate specialized queries.

**High-ROI Keyword Clusters:**

| Keyword Focus | Search Intent | Content Strategy |
|---|---|---|
| "Spinal cord injury exercise physiology [city]" | Medical, high-intent | Landing page + blog series on SCI recovery phases |
| "NDIS exercise physiology [condition]" | Funding-aware, conversion-focused | NDIS-specific pricing, how-to-access guide |
| "Exercise physiology for [condition] online" | Accessibility-focused, remote | Telehealth landing page with scheduling |
| "Disabled-friendly exercise programs [city]" | Inclusive, accessibility | Showcase accessible facilities + programs |
| "Neurological rehabilitation exercise [condition]" | Specialist-seeking | Blog: "Neuroplasticity in SCI Recovery" |

**Content Delivery Model (Hub-and-Spoke):**
- **Hub:** Condition-focused pillar pages (e.g., "SCI Rehabilitation Guide")
- **Spokes:** Blog posts linking back (e.g., "SCI Home Exercises," "Neurological Recovery Timeline")
- **Conversion:** Each spoke links to service page with booking CTA

## SECTION 4: SOCIAL MEDIA MARKETING STRATEGY

### 4.1 Platform Prioritization

**Platform 1: Facebook (Highest ROI for Disability Services)**
- NDIS participants actively use Facebook for service discovery
- Disability advocacy groups are highly engaged communities
- Making Strides proves the model (7,284 followers, high engagement)

**Content Strategy:**
- **50% Educational:** SCI recovery tips, exercise demonstrations, nutrition, mental health
- **25% Community:** Success stories, testimonials, client journeys (with consent)
- **15% Promotional:** Service announcements, special offers, event invitations
- **10% Behind-the-scenes:** Team profiles, facility tours, research updates

**Posting Cadence:** 3-4x per week (consistent presence beats sporadic large posts)

**Platform 2: Instagram (Brand Building + Reach)**
- Fitness/health niche is Instagram's sweet spot
- Short-form video (Reels) = highest engagement in 2026
- Target: Showcase exercise techniques, progress videos, team expertise

**Content Format Mix:**
- 40% Reels (15-30 sec exercise demos with captions)
- 30% Carousel posts (before/after, exercise progressions, case studies)
- 20% Stories (daily tips, Q&A, polls)
- 10% Educational graphics (infographics about conditions, recovery phases)

**Platform 3: LinkedIn (B2B + Referral Authority)**
- Target GPs, OTs, other physiotherapists for referrals
- Position as thought leader in SCI/disability rehabilitation
- Share research, case studies, industry insights
- Posting: 1-2x per week (quality over frequency)

**Platform 4: YouTube (Long-Form Authority + SEO)**
- Create condition-specific video playlists
- "SCI Recovery Exercise Series" (12-24 videos, 5-15 min each)
- Embed on website pages (increases time-on-site, helps SEO)
- Link to services pages in video descriptions

### 4.2 NDIS-Specific Social Content Ideas

**High-Performing Content Types:**
1. **"What is NDIS?" explainers** — Many people don't understand eligibility
2. **Funding pathway guides** — "How to get exercise physiology funded through NDIS"
3. **Success stories** — Real client journeys (anonymized)
4. **Practitioner credentials** — Build trust via qualifications + experience
5. **Accessibility features** — Highlight wheelchair access, flexible hours, telehealth
6. **FAQ content** — Address common questions (cost, duration, outcomes)

**Sample Posts:**
- "Did you know? NDIS covers up to 10 sessions/month for physiology. Here's how to access it..."
- "Client spotlight: [Name] recovered [X] function in [Y] months. Here's their story..."
- "Common myths about SCI recovery: [myth 1, 2, 3] — Let's debunk them"

### 4.3 Community Building

**Key Tactic:** Join + be active in Facebook disability groups
- Spinal Cord Injury support groups (QLD-based)
- NDIS participant groups
- Disability services provider networks
- Don't sell; provide helpful advice, build credibility

**Expected ROI:** 3-6 months of consistent community engagement = steady referral stream from word-of-mouth

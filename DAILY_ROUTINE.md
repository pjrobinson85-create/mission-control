# Daily Routine Framework — Paul Robinson

**Updated:** 2026-03-02 (Post-rugby active recovery + flexible bowel days)

## Energy Framework

| Level | Definition | Typical Times | Best For |
|-------|-----------|--------------|----------|
| **High** | Sharp, clear focus, fast thinking | 8:30–10:30 AM (always) | Task initiation, complex work, decision-making |
| **Medium** | Steady, can handle routine tasks | 10:30 AM–12:30 PM | Emails, admin, light research, meetings |
| **Variable** | Could be hyperfocus OR fatigue | 1:00–5:00 PM | Depends on sleep/stress—plan for either |
| **Low** | Struggling to start, need reminders | 5:00 PM onward | Reading, light messages, planning, rest |
| **Crashed** | Post-bowel/post-rugby fatigue | Hours 2-4 after routine/rugby | Recovery only—light tasks, movement, water |

  - Defaults: NDIS research, Report Writer code, complex decisions

  - Defaults: Email responses, admin, light research

  - If fatigued: Light tasks, reading, breaks
- **5:00–7:00 PM** — Evening meds, dinner, wind down
- **7:00 PM+** (LOW) — Rest, reading, light creative work

## Task Batching by Energy Level

### **High Energy (8:30–10:30 AM)**
**Default tasks (in priority order):**
1. NDIS Review — Email Lynne, gather evidence, write summaries
2. Report Writer — Code review, complex logic, testing
3. Remote Testing Access — Research, architecture decisions, planning
4. Important decisions (anything requiring clear thinking)

**Rules:**
- No emails/messages until 10:30 AM (protect focus)
- One task at a time (no task-switching)
- Break at 30 mins (5-min stretch, water)
- If hyperfocused, let it ride (just remember meds/water)

---

### **Medium Energy (10:30 AM–12:30 PM)**
**Default tasks:**
1. Email responses (Paul's inbox + project partners)
2. Admin (calendar, task updates, notes)
3. Light research (tools, options, validation)
4. Light creative work (brainstorming, sketches)

**Rules:**
- Batch similar tasks (answer all emails, then admin, then research)
- 45-min focus blocks with 5-min breaks
- One decision max per hour (avoid decision fatigue)

---

### **Variable (1:00–5:00 PM)**
**Decision tree:**
- Hyperfocused? → Feed it (deep work, big problems)
- Fatigued? → Light tasks (reading, messages, admin)
- Unsure? → Default to light tasks (safer bet)

**Light tasks (if fatigued):**
- Reading documentation/research
- Reviewing feedback/comments
- Light admin
- Planning tomorrow

**Deep work (if hyperfocused):**
- Any of the high-energy defaults
- Code refactoring, complex analysis
- Exploratory research

---

### **Low Energy (5:00 PM onward)**
**Defaults:**
- Dinner, meds, evening routine
- Light reading or creative breaks
- Planning/journaling for tomorrow
- Rest, no work

## Decision Paralysis Protection

**If you're stuck ("I don't know what to do"):**

1. **Check the time:**
   - High energy (8:30–10:30 AM)? → Do Task 1 on the list above
   - Medium energy (10:30 AM–12:30 PM)? → Do emails first
   - Variable (1–5 PM)? → Do a light task (reading)
   - Low energy (5 PM+)? → Rest

2. **If still stuck:** Message me
   - I'll give you 2 options (max)
   - Pick one and go
   - No thinking required

## Context Switching Rules

**Protected focus time:**
- 8:30–10:30 AM = **NO interruptions**
  - Meds taken? Check.
  - Phone silent? Yes.
  - Telegram browser closed? Yes.
  - One task only.

**Allowed interruptions:**
- Carer needs (medical, assistance)
- Urgent messages (only from Paul's contact list)
- Meds/water reminders (every 45 mins)

**Unprotected time:**
- 10:30 AM–5:00 PM = Normal interruptions okay
- 5:00 PM+ = Expect interruptions (evening routine)
